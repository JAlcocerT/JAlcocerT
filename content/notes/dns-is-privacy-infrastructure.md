DNS is not a boring network default.

It controls what names resolve, which queries leak, where filtering happens, and how services become discoverable. In a homelab, DNS becomes privacy infrastructure, routing infrastructure, and debugging infrastructure at the same time.

Changing DNS changes who can observe your intent.
