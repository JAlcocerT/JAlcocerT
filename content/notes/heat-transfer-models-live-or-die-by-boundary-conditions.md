---
title: "Heat transfer models live or die by boundary conditions"
date: 2026-05-07
tags: ["heat-transfer", "thermodynamics", "simulation"]
description: "The heat equation is only useful when boundary and initial conditions match the real system."
---

The heat equation is not enough by itself.

A thermal model becomes useful when its boundary conditions are credible: external temperature, solar input, convection coefficients, material layers, humidity, insulation, internal heat sources, and time-varying loads.

Bad boundary conditions can make a mathematically correct heat model practically wrong.
