---
title: "Maps are decision interfaces"
date: 2026-02-21
tags: ["geospatial", "maps", "decision-making"]
description: "Maps become useful when they connect location data to choices and constraints."
---

Maps are not just visualizations of place.

They become decision interfaces when they reveal constraints: distance, time, density, routes, boundaries, weather, costs, risk, and accessibility. The map should help someone choose, compare, or plan.

The value is not the basemap. It is the spatial decision.
